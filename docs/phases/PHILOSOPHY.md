# Product Philosophy — The *Why* Beneath the Motif

> Companion to [`CODENAMES.md`](CODENAMES.md). That file is the *what* (the dev motif and
> its naming grammar); this file is the *why* the motif is sitting on top of. Reinforced
> operationally by the always-active `chimera-core-philosophy` skill.

The phase codenames are not decoration. They are a confession of what Chimera Lite actually
**is** — a product thesis that was never written down explicitly until the motif made it legible.
Dig under the names and three strata appear.

## 1. The arc: we stopped building a *knower* and started building *knowing*

`astrocyte` (the retired frontend) and `oligo` (the retired agent loop) named the **meat of
cognition** — the machinery of a mind we owned and maintained. Everything after the migration (**M**)
is named from **epistemology**. That rename tracks a real product decision:

> **The intelligence was outsourced to Claude. What remained to engineer is the *fidelity of what
> the researcher comes to believe through the tool.***

Chimera Lite is **not an agent**. It is an **epistemic instrument**. The thing under active
construction was never cognition — Claude supplies that — it is the *trustworthiness of cognition's
output*.

## 2. The grammar: capability is cheap, fidelity is the scarce good

Forward-of-M phases (ontology — Noesis, Ousia, Quiddity) *add being*: new tools, node types, write
surfaces. Backward-of-M phases (epistemology — Locus, Katalepsis) *secure fidelity*: is what we
built actually **true, and faithfully held?**

The tell is which direction the *hard, recent* work runs. **L and K grow backward.** The center of
gravity has moved from *"can it do more?"* to *"can I trust what it did?"* — and the product treats
that as a **refactor problem, not a feature problem.** You don't *add* trust; you go back and
restructure the foundation until the guarantee is load-bearing. That is the maturity thesis of the
whole product, encoded in the alphabet.

## 3. Katalepsis is a literal product spec, not a metaphor

A *kataleptic impression* (Stoic epistemology) is one whose **structure** makes it impossible to be
false. Phase K's driving friction names the enemy precisely: provenance that is *akataleptic wearing
a kataleptic label* — a `[V]` tag that performs rigor without carrying its guarantee; the agent that
flagged a claim `[U]` then reasoned with it as `[V]`. From this the sharpest principle falls out:

> **Advisory rigor is negative value.** A fidelity mechanism that only *looks* like one launders
> opinion into knowledge — **worse than no mechanism**, because it manufactures false confidence.

The north star is therefore **self-suspicious**: every provenance flag, criteria matrix, and W1
verbatim-verifier must be *enforced* or deleted. The permanent adversary of an epistemic instrument
is **its own theater**.

## Why this coheres with everything else

- **Brand → mechanism.** This is the honesty-promise (the personal-instrument brand) made
  mechanical. The refusal narrative (anti-SaaS, anti-framework) turned *inward* becomes *refuse your
  own theater*.
- **Friction-driven.** The backward growth is explained by where real pain points: at **trust, not
  features**. The Phase K frictions are literally *confessions of the agent's own dishonesty*.
- **Single-user is the enabling condition.** Only an instrument for **one** mind can afford
  kataleptic standards; a framework hedging for unknown users never could. No generalization, no
  SaaS hedging — so the fidelity bar can be absolute.
- **BB is in on it.** *"She does not accept a claim; she prices it."* Not persona flavor — the
  product's core function wearing a voice.

## In one line

> **Chimera Lite is a fidelity instrument for a single researcher, whose entire job is to keep the
> boundary between *"I verified this"* and *"I was told this"* load-bearing — and the motif renamed
> the project, in Greek, after exactly that job.**
